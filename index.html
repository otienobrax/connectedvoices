Who are We?

CONNECTED VOICES
Connected Voices is a non-profit organization that provides a voice for people who have lost theirs. We plan on achieving 1 Million Donations.

  • Our Aim

    Despite the incredible and sometimes heroic efforts, too many individuals and communities around the world are living in disability, hopelessness, and being denied a voice. These individuals deserve better. We are Connected Voices for Hope - a campaign to raise awareness about the voiceless and provide support for them. There are millions in the world who have been silenced because of their disability, gender, and minority status. For a $1.00 donation together with your help, we can offer life-changing innovations and change their story.

  • Our Vision

    We envision a society where those who are disabled due to the condition they have are given the opportunity to showcase their skills, and as life calls for it, find their way back into everyday practice.

  • You'll be heard

    Whether you're looking for support, want to offer your voice, want to Sponsor the organization, or Donate items to help improve the lives of these individuals, Connected Voices has it all.

  • Objective

    The objective of this project is to provide support together with other similar non profit organizations for people with spinal cord injuries by organizing concerts that will help raise funds, provide equipment and donations that will alleviate their suffering and improve their quality of life.

RESEARCH AND DEVELOPMENT

(i.) Dancing Molecules

For decades, scientists have looked for an effective treatment for spinal cord injuries, often with little success. New research by Northwestern University scientists has resulted in a game-changing innovation: an injection that uses ​“dancing molecules” to repair spinal tissue and reverse paralysis

(II.) Robotics and AI

Artificial intelligence has tremendous potential in revolutionizing comprehensive spine care. Evidence-based, predictive analytics can help surgeons improve preoperative patient selection, surgical indications, and individualized postoperative care. Robotic-assisted surgery, while still in the early stages of development, has the potential to reduce surgeon fatigue and improve technical precision.

It's the possibility that keeps us going.
